Political Science and Code? An unusual path.

I attended the political science programm at the Goethe University in Frankfurt. As my secondary subject I chose American Studies. My Englisch benefited tremendously from my secondary subject. My Bachelors Thesis is written in Englisch eventough my first language is German. In total I completed the bulk of my studies in Englisch.

In my main course of study I was introduced to a wide variety of subjects. Philosophy, political theory, political economics, irnternational relations, different research methodologeis but most importantly I was introduced to statistics and statistical analysis. In the statistical analysis courses tought in R, SPSS and STATA I discovered that I love to code, to solve problems using it and find answers to questions using data. Data is unbelivably useful and I like to put it to work.

This culminated in my bachelors thesis in which I used R and the Eurobarometer 92.3 to answer two questions targeting attitudes towards the EU. My thesis is identitiy focuesed as this research area is underresearched an curcial to attitudes towards the EU. If I catched your interest you can download my paper below.

Through the statistical coding I discovered Python and coding in general. I expanded my knowledge and my interest only grew. Now I know how to work with Python, FastApi, Tailwindcss and R with its different packages (just to name a few). I want to know more, gain more indepth knowledge and work in the field. I had a passion for computers all my life but finally discovered how interesting software and code can be as I allready did with hardware. If I peaked your interest get in touch with me.
